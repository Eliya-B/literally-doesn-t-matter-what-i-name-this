History of Space Exploration

Space exploration began in earnest during the mid-20th century with the launch of Sputnik 1 by the Soviet Union in 1957 — the first artificial satellite to orbit Earth. This event marked the beginning of the Space Age and the Space Race between the USSR and the USA.

Famous Space Missions

  • Apollo 11 (1969): First manned Moon landing.
  • Voyager 1 (1977): Now the farthest human-made object in space.
  • Hubble Space Telescope (1990): Revolutionized our view of the universe.
  • Perseverance Rover (2021): Exploring the surface of Mars.

The Future of Space Travel

The next frontier includes human missions to Mars, lunar bases, and even asteroid mining. Private companies like SpaceX and Blue Origin are driving innovation and making space more accessible than ever before.
